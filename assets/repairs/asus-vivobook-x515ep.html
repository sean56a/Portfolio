Asus VivoBook X515EP

A second-hand ASUS VivoBook X515EP acquired with existing cosmetic and hardware-related concerns. The device was inspected, maintained, and restored through hands-on troubleshooting, internal cleaning, and hardware servicing to improve reliability and extend its usable lifespan.

2026 ~2 hours Resolved

01 Overview

A second-hand ASUS VivoBook X515EP was acquired with signs of aging and reduced performance during daily usage. Due to its previous condition and lack of documented maintenance history, the device underwent inspection and preventive maintenance to improve thermal performance and overall reliability.

02 Problem

The laptop experienced increased fan noise and elevated operating temperatures during normal workloads. Performance degradation was suspected due to accumulated dust and aging thermal compound.

03 Diagnosis

Thermal behavior was monitored using HWMonitor to evaluate CPU temperature performance. Inspection revealed dust buildup around the cooling system and degraded thermal compound, reducing heat transfer efficiency between the CPU and heatsink.

04 Tools Used

  • Precision screwdriver set
  • Anti-static wrist strap
  • Thermal paste (high-performance)
  • Compressed air / soft brush
  • Isopropyl alcohol & lint-free cloth
  • HWMonitor for temperature diagnostics

05 Repair Process

  • Disassembled the laptop following proper safety procedures.
  • Removed accumulated dust from the fan and heatsink assembly.
  • Cleaned the CPU and heatsink contact surfaces.
  • Applied fresh thermal compound.
  • Reassembled the device and verified proper operation.

06 Testing

Performed temperature monitoring after maintenance to verify improved thermal behavior during normal workloads.

07 Outcome

The ASUS VivoBook X515EP achieved improved cooling performance, reduced fan activity, and more stable operation during remote work tasks.

08 Lessons Learned

This restoration reinforced the importance of preventive maintenance when handling used hardware. Proper cleaning, thermal servicing, and systematic inspection can significantly improve device reliability and prevent future failures.
