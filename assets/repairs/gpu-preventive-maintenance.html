Graphics Card (GPU) — Preventive Maintenance

Preventive maintenance performed on an AMD Radeon RX 580 2048SP to restore cooling performance through a complete teardown, deep cleaning, and replacement of aged thermal paste and thermal pads.

2026 ~1 hour Resolved

01 Overview

An AMD Radeon RX 580 2048SP graphics card, a widely used budget-oriented GPU, was brought in for preventive maintenance after several years of continuous use with no previous internal servicing. The owner reported increasing operating temperatures during extended gaming sessions.

02 Problem

During prolonged gaming, GPU temperatures steadily increased, causing the cooling fans to operate at high speeds. The elevated temperatures occasionally resulted in thermal throttling, reducing overall gaming performance.

03 Diagnosis

Temperature monitoring using GPU-Z and MSI Afterburner showed the GPU core operating significantly hotter than expected under sustained load. Considering the card's age and maintenance history, the most probable causes were degraded thermal paste and accumulated dust restricting efficient heat dissipation. The existing thermal pads were inspected during disassembly and remained in good condition, requiring no replacement.

04 Tools Used

  • Precision screwdriver set
  • Anti-static wrist strap
  • Thermal paste (high-performance)
  • Compressed air
  • Isopropyl alcohol & lint-free cloth
  • GPU-Z & MSI Afterburner for monitoring and stress testing

05 Repair Process

  • Removed the graphics card from the system and disconnected all power connectors.
  • Carefully unscrewed and lifted off the fan shroud and heatsink assembly to expose the PCB.
  • Cleaned heavy dust buildup from the heatsink fins and fan blades using compressed air and a soft brush.
  • Removed the old, dried thermal paste from the GPU die using isopropyl alcohol and a lint-free cloth.
  • Applied fresh thermal paste to the GPU die. The existing thermal pads were inspected for wear and compression, found to be serviceable, and retained during reassembly.
  • Reassembled the heatsink and fan shroud, checking fan cable connections before final closure.
  • Reinstalled the card and reconnected all power cabling.

06 Testing

Performed a sustained GPU stress test using BurnInTest while monitoring core temperature, fan speed, and overall system stability. Prior to maintenance, the GPU reached approximately 89–90°C under full load. Following cleaning and thermal paste replacement, peak operating temperature decreased to approximately 77–78°C, with stable clock speeds and no thermal throttling observed throughout the test.

07 Outcome

Preventive maintenance successfully improved the graphics card's cooling efficiency, reducing peak operating temperatures by approximately 12°C under sustained load. The GPU now maintains stable performance with lower fan noise and improved thermal headroom during extended gaming sessions.

08 Lessons Learned

Preventive maintenance should always begin with inspection rather than replacing parts by default. In this case, the thermal paste had degraded and required replacement, while the factory thermal pads remained in good condition and continued to provide proper contact with the VRAM and VRM components.
