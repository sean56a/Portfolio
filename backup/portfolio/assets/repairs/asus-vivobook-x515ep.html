Asus VivoBook X515EP

Overheating and unexpected shutdowns under normal usage, traced to degraded thermal compound and heavy dust buildup.

2025 ~2 hours Resolved
device-photo-placeholder.jpg

01 Overview

A client-owned Asus VivoBook X515EP was brought in after repeatedly shutting down during everyday use such as browsing and document editing. The unit was roughly three years old with no prior maintenance history.

02 Problem

The laptop would shut down without warning after 20–40 minutes of use, worsening under any sustained CPU load. Fan noise was noticeably louder than normal, especially in the minutes before each shutdown.

03 Diagnosis

Using HWMonitor, CPU temperatures were observed climbing past 95°C within minutes of load, well above safe thresholds, while idle temperatures were also higher than expected for the model. This pointed to a cooling system failure rather than a software or power issue.

04 Tools Used

  • Precision screwdriver set
  • Anti-static wrist strap
  • Thermal paste (high-performance)
  • Compressed air / soft brush
  • Isopropyl alcohol & lint-free cloth
  • HWMonitor for temperature diagnostics

05 Repair Process

  • Disconnected battery before opening the chassis to prevent shorts.
  • Removed the bottom panel and located the heatsink and fan assembly.
  • Found the heatsink fins packed with dust, restricting airflow almost entirely.
  • Cleaned the fan blades and heatsink fins thoroughly with compressed air and a soft brush.
  • Removed the old, dried-out thermal paste from the CPU die and heatsink contact plate using isopropyl alcohol.
  • Applied a fresh, even layer of high-performance thermal paste.
  • Reassembled the unit and reconnected the battery.

06 Testing

Ran a stress test using CPU load for 30 minutes while monitoring temperatures. Peak temperatures dropped from 95°C+ to a stable 72–78°C under sustained load, with no shutdowns observed. Fan noise also returned to normal levels.

07 Outcome

The laptop now runs full workloads without thermal shutdown. The client was advised on a 6–12 month cleaning interval to prevent recurrence.

08 Lessons Learned

Thermal issues are among the most common — and most preventable — laptop failures. This repair reinforced the value of a systematic before/after temperature comparison to actually confirm a fix, rather than assuming success from a visual cleaning alone.
